Welcome

Hi, I'm Aisha Bigham

Data Science & Psychology | Minor in Immersive Media @ Northeastern University

I'm a Data Science and Psychology major at Northeastern University with a minor in Immersive Media. I'm passionate about exploring how data, technology, and human behavior intersect — especially within virtual environments. My work bridges VR development, cognitive research, and data analytics to study how people perceive and interact in immersive digital worlds.

My research spans both Khoury College of Computer Sciences + CAMD and the College of Science. In one project, accepted to ISMAR 2025, I study gaze behavior and field dependency in VR to understand how individual differences influence perception. In another, I investigate spatial cognition in VR, including running participants, collecting behavioral data, and troubleshooting Unity-based experiments using the Meta Quest headset.

Alongside research, I teach as a Coding & Game Development Instructor at Code Wiz, where I develop lesson plans and built a Unity 3D curriculum entirely from scratch. Working with students has deepened my appreciation for making complex technical concepts accessible and engaging.

My goal is to build experiences that are both data-driven and deeply human. From analyzing eye-tracking data to designing VR experiments to teaching kids to code, I approach every project with curiosity and intention. I see the future of technology living at the intersection of cognitive science and immersive media, and I am proud to be doing work right at that edge.
